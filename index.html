Nashville Long Weekend Guide

May 1–4, 2026 • Staying at Embassy Suites Nashville

Overview

A long weekend in Nashville during early May means peak music season, warm weather, and a perfect mix of classic venues, chef-driven dining, and walkable neighborhoods. This guide highlights must-see attractions, concerts, regional food, and restaurants— all mapped for quick orientation from your base at Embassy Suites by Hilton Nashville Downtown.

Must‑See Attractions

Music & Culture

  • Ryman Auditorium Tour Downtown
  • Country Music Hall of Fame & Museum Museum Downtown
  • RCA Studio B Studio Tour Downtown
  • Johnny Cash Museum Museum Downtown
  • Grand Ole Opry House Show Outskirts

History & Landmarks

  • The Parthenon at Centennial Park Landmark Midtown
  • Belle Meade Historic Site & Winery Historic Outskirts
  • Andrew Jackson’s Hermitage Historic Outskirts

Outdoors & Scenic

  • Cheekwood Estate & Gardens Gardens Outskirts
  • Riverfront Park Park Downtown
  • Radnor Lake State Park Hiking Outskirts

Events & Concerts (Weekend Highlights)

  • Grand Ole Opry – Classic multi-artist country show at the Opry House Friday
  • Ani DiFranco – Live at the Ryman Auditorium Friday
  • Bailey Zimmerman – Ascend Amphitheater, riverfront outdoor show Friday/Saturday
  • Nashville Symphony – “The Miraculous Mandarin & Music of Bates” at Schermerhorn Symphony Center Friday
  • Florence + The Machine – Bridgestone Arena Saturday
  • Local clubs & comedy – Zanies, The Basement, 3rd & Lindsley throughout the weekend

Regional Food & Restaurants

Must‑Try Regional Dishes

  • Hot Chicken – Nashville’s signature spicy fried chicken.
  • Meat & Three – One protein, three Southern sides.
  • Nashville‑style BBQ – Pork-forward, tangy sauces.
  • Biscuits & Gravy – Classic Southern breakfast.
  • Goo Goo Cluster – Historic Nashville candy.

Hot Chicken

  • Prince’s Hot Chicken Outskirts
  • Hattie B’s Hot Chicken (Midtown) Midtown

Chef‑Driven & Chef’s‑Table Experiences

  • The Catbird Seat – Chef’s counter tasting menu Midtown
  • Yolan – Upscale Italian by Tony Mantuano Downtown
  • Kayne Prime – Modern steakhouse Midtown
  • Husk – Modern Southern in a historic mansion Downtown
  • Locust – Minimalist, inventive tasting menu 12South/Belmont
  • Bastion – Creative small tasting menu 12South/Belmont

Southern Comfort & Classics

  • The Loveless Café – Legendary biscuits Outskirts
  • Arnold’s Country Kitchen – Meat & three Downtown

Cocktails & Nightlife

  • The Patterson House – Speakeasy cocktails Midtown
  • Skull’s Rainbow Room – Supper club with jazz & burlesque Downtown

Suggested Daily Plan

Friday, May 1

Downtown

  • Check in at Embassy Suites Nashville Downtown.
  • Explore Broadway and Riverfront Park; rooftop drink at White Limozeen or L27.
  • Dinner at Husk or Yolan.
  • Evening: Ani DiFranco at Ryman, Grand Ole Opry, or Nashville Symphony.

Saturday, May 2

OutskirtsMidtownDowntown

  • Breakfast at The Loveless Café.
  • Visit Cheekwood Estate & Gardens.
  • Afternoon at Country Music Hall of Fame & RCA Studio B.
  • Dinner at The Catbird Seat (chef’s table).
  • Evening: Florence + The Machine (Bridgestone) or Bailey Zimmerman (Ascend Amphitheater).

Sunday, May 3

12SouthMidtownDowntown

  • Brunch at Henrietta Red or Butcher & Bee.
  • Explore the 12South neighborhood (murals, boutiques, coffee).
  • Visit Centennial Park & The Parthenon.
  • Dinner at Bastion or Locust.
  • Low‑key live music at 3rd & Lindsley or The Basement.

Monday, May 4

Downtown

  • Coffee at Frothy Monkey.
  • Walk along Riverfront Park.
  • Pick up Goo Goo Clusters and depart.

Good to Know

  • Book concerts and The Catbird Seat early – they sell out quickly in May.
  • Use rideshare – parking downtown is pricey and limited.
  • Hot chicken strategy – go off‑peak (late lunch) to avoid long lines.
  • Weather – warm days, cooler evenings; pack layers and comfortable shoes.
