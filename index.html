Nashville Hot Chicken

Nashville's most famous dish ‚Äî fried chicken seasoned with a cayenne-heavy spice paste applied post-fry, served on white bread with pickles. It's fiery, addictive, and deeply local. Heat levels range from plain to 'shut the cluck up.' Always order one level below what you think you can handle.

Know Before You Go

Insider Tips & Logistics

Broadway Reality Check
Broadway gets very loud and very crowded by 9 PM on weekends. If you dislike crowds, arrive before 7 PM or plan honky-tonk time earlier in the evening. The 'Nash-Vegas' bachelorette party scene is real and abundant.
Escape the Tourist Zone
For a more authentic local experience, venture to East Nashville, Germantown, or 12 South. The bars and restaurants there are where locals actually spend their time ‚Äî and the food is often better.
Honky-Tonk Etiquette
Lower Broadway bars are free to enter, but the musicians work entirely for tips. Tip generously ‚Äî they're often extraordinarily talented professionals who have simply chosen the bar stage over the touring circuit.
The Ryman Experience
The Ryman's acoustics are genuinely legendary. Even a mediocre show sounds incredible in that room. If you can catch any show there, do it. The backstage tour is also worth the hour.
Weather
May weather is ideal but bring a compact umbrella. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in Tennessee but usually brief and clear quickly. Evenings can be cool ‚Äî pack a light layer for outdoor venues.
Getting Around
Uber/Lyft surge hard on weekend nights near Broadway. Order your ride a block or two away and a few minutes early. Alternatively, the B-Cycle bike share is great for daytime neighborhood-hopping.
